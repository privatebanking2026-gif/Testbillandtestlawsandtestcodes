Seventhly, as to the promises, acknowledgments, or payments, which take a bill or note out of the statute.

It was at first held that nothing short of an express promise would take a debt out of the statute; then that a mere acknowledgment would, as evidence of a promise; and that a part payment of principal or interest amounted to an acknowledgment.
